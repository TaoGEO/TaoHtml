SHARED ROOM FIELD STUDY
Synthetic business fixture. It contains no real organization or personal data.

Study question
How often were reserved shared rooms visibly unused during the sampled periods?

Observation design
Two buildings were observed for five business days. Researchers checked 12 shared rooms at 10:00, 14:00, and 16:00. A slot was counted as "reserved and visibly unused" when the booking display showed an active reservation and no occupant was visible during a three-minute observation window.

The observation does not establish why a room was empty. It cannot distinguish late arrival, a brief absence, an incorrect display, or a reservation that should have been released.

Field-sheet note
One North observation sheet labels the final check as 15:30, while the study protocol says 16:00. The 12 slots on that sheet are included in the totals above. The time-label discrepancy should remain visible until the field team reconciles it.

INTERPRETATION BOUNDARY

The study shows that reserved rooms were sometimes visibly unused during sampled windows. It does not prove that the current automatic-release rule caused the observations.

A follow-up could compare rooms with and without reminder prompts, record actual check-in events, and reconcile the 15:30 versus 16:00 field-sheet label before estimating an effect.
